# The Prompt Economy Playbook — Revised Instructional Magazine

## Editorial direction

This revised version should feel **cleaner, softer, and more useful**. The design should keep a subtle Bauhaus influence, but remove the loud poster energy. Use a quieter palette, more whitespace, fewer words, stronger image moments, and simple step-by-step teaching.

### Visual system

| Element | Direction |
| --- | --- |
| **Mood** | Minimal editorial magazine, calm, premium, instructional |
| **Palette** | Oat, charcoal, muted brick, dusty blue, warm beige |
| **Typography** | Bold headline, light body, lots of breathing room |
| **Imagery** | More abstract editorial images, cropped website fragments, simple diagrams, document-style visuals |
| **Layout** | One idea per page, 3 to 4 steps max, large margins, generous whitespace |

The core rule is simple: **teach, do not overload**.

---

## Slide 1 — This playbook shows you how to get found by AI agents

**Purpose:** Open with a calm, confident cover that explains what the reader will learn.

**Teaching angle:** This is not theory. It is a step-by-step guide for making a business easier for AI systems to find, trust, and use.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
How to get your business found by AI agents

### Subtitle
A simple step-by-step playbook for the prompt economy

### Short support line
If an AI cannot understand your business, it cannot recommend it or use it.

### Mini steps
1. Make your business easy to find.
2. Make it easy to understand.
3. Make it easy to trust.
4. Make it easy to act on.

**Visual direction:** Magazine cover with one strong abstract image, soft geometric forms, lots of empty space, and a quiet editorial feel.

## Slide 2 — Step 1: Start with the pages people and models actually need

**Purpose:** Teach the reader what pages to create first.

**Teaching angle:** Instead of relying on one homepage, build a few task pages that answer real buying questions.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 1: Create pages for real tasks

### Short intro
Do not ask AI to guess what your business does. Give it clear pages for the tasks customers actually want to complete.

### Step-by-step
1. List the top 3 to 5 actions a customer wants to take.
2. Create one clear page for each action.
3. Give each page a simple URL and one job.

### Example table
| Action | Page to create |
| --- | --- |
| See prices | `/pricing` |
| Book a service | `/book` |
| Compare an offer | `/services/design-audit` |
| Ask how agents can use you | `/agents` |

### Bottom line
Think in tasks, not just brand pages.

**Visual direction:** Use a calm collage of page blocks or URL cards, with one supporting abstract image and lots of whitespace.

## Slide 3 — Step 2: Write each page like an answer, not an ad

**Purpose:** Show exactly how to structure page copy.

**Teaching angle:** AI systems prefer clear answers over vague marketing language.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 2: Write each page like a clear answer

### Short intro
Every important page should answer the same five questions right away.

### Step-by-step
1. Who is this for?
2. What can they do here?
3. What does it cost or require?
4. What are the limits or rules?
5. What should they do next?

### Simple example
| Weak line | Better line |
| --- | --- |
| We help teams unlock growth | Book a 30-minute growth audit for SaaS teams with 10 to 200 employees |

### Bottom line
If a sentence sounds clever but unclear, rewrite it.

**Visual direction:** Large simple typography, one editorial image, and a clean five-part content scaffold.

## Slide 4 — Step 3: Make the most important facts visible without clicking around

**Purpose:** Teach what information must be visible on-page.

**Teaching angle:** AI and humans both lose confidence when essential information is hidden.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 3: Put the key facts in plain sight

### Short intro
Your page should work even if someone only scans the top half.

### Step-by-step
1. Show pricing or pricing logic if possible.
2. State what is included and what is not.
3. Add timing, location, or eligibility rules.
4. Keep one obvious next action.

### Checklist block
| Show this clearly | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Price or price range | Helps comparison |
| What is included | Reduces confusion |
| Rules or limits | Builds trust |
| One next step | Reduces drop-off |

### Bottom line
Clear pages convert better because they reduce uncertainty.

**Visual direction:** Minimal instructional layout with one supporting image and a simple checklist treatment.

## Slide 5 — Step 4: Publish the discovery files that help AI find your best pages

**Purpose:** Teach the machine-discovery layer in a simple way.

**Teaching angle:** These files are basic routing tools, not technical decoration.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 4: Add the discovery layer

### Short intro
Once your core pages are ready, help AI systems find them faster and more reliably.

### Step-by-step
1. Keep `robots.txt` intentional.
2. Publish a clean `sitemap.xml`.
3. Add `llms.txt` with links to your best pages.
4. Keep your key docs public.

### Simple file guide
| File | What it should do |
| --- | --- |
| `robots.txt` | Tell crawlers what they can access |
| `sitemap.xml` | List your important pages |
| `llms.txt` | Point AI systems to the best business context |

### Bottom line
Make your best information easy to locate.

**Visual direction:** Use clean document-style imagery, light diagrams, and subtle geometric navigation motifs.

## Slide 6 — Step 5: Align your facts everywhere

**Purpose:** Teach consistency across site copy, schema, and business profiles.

**Teaching angle:** The same business facts should appear the same way everywhere.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 5: Keep your facts consistent everywhere

### Short intro
AI trusts businesses that say the same thing in the same way across every surface.

### Step-by-step
1. Match your website copy to your real offer.
2. Match your structured data to the visible page.
3. Match your business profiles to your website.
4. Match your agent docs to what your system can really do.

### Alignment table
| Surface | Check this |
| --- | --- |
| Website | Offer, price, policy, support |
| Schema | Same visible facts |
| Business listings | Name, domain, phone, hours |
| Agent docs | Real actions and limits |

### Bottom line
Consistency makes your business easier to trust.

**Visual direction:** Calm grid layout with one abstract reference image and neat alignment lines.

## Slide 7 — Step 6: Give AI a page that explains how to use your business

**Purpose:** Teach the reader how to create an agent access page.

**Teaching angle:** This is where discoverability turns into action.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 6: Publish an agent access page

### Short intro
If you want AI to do something with your business, show exactly how that works.

### Step-by-step
1. Create a page like `/agents` or `/docs/agent-access`.
2. List what actions are supported.
3. Explain login or approval rules.
4. Add help or fallback paths.

### What to include
| Section | Add this |
| --- | --- |
| Supported actions | Booking, lookup, browse, support |
| Access rules | What needs login |
| Approval rules | What needs human confirmation |
| Help path | Where to go if automation stops |

### Bottom line
Do not make AI guess how to interact with you.

**Visual direction:** Cleaner action-flow composition with one stronger illustrative image and simple pathway graphics.

## Slide 8 — Step 7: Update your public information whenever the business changes

**Purpose:** Teach freshness as a repeatable practice.

**Teaching angle:** A stale business is harder for AI to trust.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 7: Keep your public information fresh

### Short intro
When your business changes, your machine-facing layer should change too.

### Step-by-step
1. Update changed pages first.
2. Refresh your sitemap and docs.
3. Re-run Agent-See if workflows changed.
4. Review your highest-value pages every month.

### Simple cadence
| When | Review |
| --- | --- |
| Weekly | Broken links and stale offers |
| Monthly | Core pages, schema, discovery files |
| After product changes | Runtime, docs, and task pages |

### Bottom line
Fresh information is a trust signal.

**Visual direction:** Quiet cyclical diagram, soft calendar-like visual, and minimal text blocks.

## Slide 9 — Step 8: Build a small reference library AI can cite

**Purpose:** Teach the reader what supporting pages to publish next.

**Teaching angle:** AI often prefers clear reference pages over persuasive copy.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Step 8: Build a reference layer AI can cite

### Short intro
After your core pages are live, publish a few simple reference pages that explain your business clearly.

### Step-by-step
1. Add a pricing and eligibility page.
2. Add a policies page.
3. Add examples or case studies.
4. Add support and escalation guidance.

### Suggested pages
| Page | Why it helps |
| --- | --- |
| Pricing and eligibility | Easier comparison |
| Policies | More trust |
| Examples | More proof |
| Support and escalation | Safer action |

### Bottom line
Reference pages help AI recommend you with confidence.

**Visual direction:** Editorial archive feel with more imagery, softer labels, and simple reference-card layouts.

## Slide 10 — Your operating loop for the prompt economy

**Purpose:** Close with a light, memorable framework.

**Teaching angle:** Leave the reader with a repeatable process they can actually follow.

**On-page copy:**

### Title
Your simple operating loop for the prompt economy

### Loop
1. Create clear task pages.
2. Add the discovery layer.
3. Keep facts aligned.
4. Publish an agent access page.
5. Refresh everything as the business evolves.

### Closing line
Use Agent-See to convert the business, then run the business in a way AI can find, trust, and use.

### Final takeaway
The businesses that win are not the loudest. They are the easiest to understand and act on.

**Visual direction:** Quiet final spread with a soft loop graphic, one elegant abstract image, and a calm premium ending.
